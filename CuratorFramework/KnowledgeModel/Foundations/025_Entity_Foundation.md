# 025 – Entity Foundation

Status: Draft 0.1

---

## Purpose

This document defines the ontological foundation of entities within the Curator Framework.

It describes **when** an entity comes into existence, **how** identity is established, and **how** entities evolve through assertions over time.

---

# Axiom 1

A Reference Point is not an Entity.

A Reference Point merely denotes something that may become the subject of knowledge.

It has no semantic identity by itself.

---

# Axiom 2

An Entity comes into existence through an Assertion.

Without an Assertion there is no Entity.

An Assertion establishes identity.

---

# Axiom 3

Every Assertion has

- an Agent
- Evidence
- a Context

Therefore no Entity exists independently from the circumstances under which it was asserted.

---

# Axiom 4

Identity is contextual.

Identity is never absolute.

Different identity spaces may coexist.

Examples include

- identical files
- identical works
- identical names
- alias identities
- semantic identities

None of these identities excludes another.

---

# Axiom 5

Multiple Assertions may establish the same Entity.

Each Assertion contributes additional knowledge.

No Assertion replaces another merely by existing.

---

# Axiom 6

Contradictory Assertions may coexist.

Contradiction is knowledge.

The Curator Framework preserves contradictory Assertions as part of the historical knowledge base.

---

# Axiom 7

One Assertion may become dominant.

Dominance affects presentation.

It never deletes or invalidates historical Assertions.

---

# Axiom 8

Assertions are themselves Entities.

Assertions may therefore become the subject or object of further Assertions.

Knowledge is recursively describable.

---

# Axiom 9

Entities evolve.

An Entity is not a static object.

It is the accumulated result of Assertions made throughout its lifetime.

---

# Axiom 10

Semantic knowledge emerges from the history of Assertions.

Learning is therefore not the replacement of facts.

Learning is the continuous accumulation, evaluation and reorganisation of Assertions.

---

# Practical Example

Importing a file illustrates the complete process.

Reference Point

↓

Assertion

"This is a file."

↓

Entity

↓

Further Assertions

"This file contains audio."

↓

"This recording represents Beethoven."

↓

"This recording belongs to Piano Concerto No. 5."

↓

"This interpretation is conducted by Karajan."

The Entity grows by accumulated Assertions.

Its history is never lost.

---

# Consequences

The Curator Framework never asks

"What is this object?"

Instead it asks

"Which Assertions currently establish the identity of this Entity?"

Identity is therefore an emergent property of accumulated knowledge rather than an intrinsic property of an object.
